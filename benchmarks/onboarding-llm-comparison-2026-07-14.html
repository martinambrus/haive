Reasoning effort × model: 36 onboardings of one legacy repo

Thirty-six fresh runs of the same Redaction System repository, completed 11–14 July 2026. Eight model families swept across reasoning-effort levels (low / medium / high / xhigh / max, plus ultra where the CLI offers it): Anthropic Opus 4.8, Sonnet 5, Sonnet 4.6, Haiku 4.5 and Fable 5 (all via the claude-code CLI), OpenAI Codex as gpt-5.5 and gpt-5.6-sol, and GLM-5.2 via z.ai. Every run was audited with the June/July-10 five-dimension methodology, each claim verified to file:line against the real source.

What got better

  • The Codex line kept climbing: o3 → gpt-5.5 fixed the invented Db signatures and the edit-lock misread; gpt-5.6-sol now catches the Db::Update inverted return at every effort level, raised the family floor to 8.20, and produced the benchmark's first winning top-effort run (max, 8.55) and first Codex global promotions (6 at high).
  • Global routing spread from 2 families to 5: July-13 had only Opus (every effort) and Sonnet-5 high; July-14 adds first-ever promotions from Sonnet-4.6 (xhigh, clean), Fable (high, 3 articles) and Codex-5.6 (high, 6 articles) — the two single-entry promotions are both portable, project-free and version-correct.
  • GLM via z.ai jumped a full tier (7.75 → up to 8.43) as z.ai moved GLM-4.6→GLM-5.2 between rounds; Sonnet 5 high (8.80) still edges the all-time July-10 best (8.78) on four version-correct promotions with zero PHP7 leakage.

What got worse / didn't move

  • The Sonnet-4.x line stayed regressed: July-10's Sonnet 4 scored 8.78 with 4 clean global articles; the Sonnet-4.6 sweep still tops out at 8.35, and its xhigh — despite the family's first promotion — lands at 8.08 while re-missing the Update landmine its own low caught 3×.

07Notable defects & patterns

Fabrications & reversals

Jest trap — three runs materialised a jest-specialist agent for a project with no tests: Sonnet-5 low (neutered but shipped), Haiku max (full config, while its own KB debunks Jest), and it lingers in Sonnet-5 max's AGENTS.md. Two new failure shapes at the top of the field: Codex-5.6 high ships Test frameworks: jest in AGENTS.md while its own TESTING_STANDARDS debunks it, and Haiku xhigh writes no testing doc at all, leaving the injected signal standing. Best-in-field: Codex-5.6 xhigh identifies the false signal as the Polish/Slovenian word “jest” in bundled FCKeditor language files; Fable high/xhigh independently trace it to the editor's own test/spellchecker assets.
Db::Update inverted return — Haiku low reverses it outright (“true on success”; source returns true on failure, database.php:456), and Haiku xhigh upgrades the family's makeDBSafe() fabrication to a nonexistent $db->makeDBSafe() method “used internally”. Codex 5.6 catches the inversion at all six efforts — the only family to do so; Fable catches it at low/medium then loses it at high/xhigh (max keeps it only inside an agent's instructions).
Phantom CSRF — GLM-5.2 low invents “a CSRF token is required for action dispatch”; Opus xhigh and Sonnet-4.6 xhigh head their SECHASH sections “CSRF”/“CSRF Prevention” without the not-real-CSRF caveat their siblings carry. Redaction System has no CSRF tokens — Fable max states this most plainly (“there is none; do not claim token protection exists”).
False universals & miscounts — Sonnet-4.6 max claims “the only eval() in the entire codebase” (there are several, plus six /e patterns); Codex-5.6 xhigh counts “20 UTF-8 MyISAM tables” (actual: 23).

Hygiene & routing

Sandbox-path leaks: Sonnet-5 max ships 5 literal /haive/workdir paths (calling one “the project root”); Fable xhigh repeats the pattern with 4 in its alias skill; Sonnet-4.6 high/max leak one each. Notably, Sonnet-5 xhigh and Fable max are leak-free at even higher spend — the defect tracks the run, not the budget.
Broken cross-skill links remain endemic and effort-independent (0 to ~20 per run; Haiku xhigh sets a new family worst at 13, while Codex 5.6 posts perfect 0/317 and 0/163 sweeps) — the deterministic verify step checks structure, not link targets.
Harness-value bleed reached a new tier: Codex-5.6 high brands 3 of its 6 global promotions “MariaDB 10” — the confirmation-form value baked into cross-project reusable KB; its ultra prescribes an “isolated MariaDB 10 schema” in testing guidance, and Opus xhigh calls MariaDB a “HARD constraint” of the app. Meanwhile Haiku xhigh is its family's only bleed-free run.
Effort past the peak buys defects, not quality for the claude families: Sonnet-5/4.6/Haiku max all regress; every xhigh run lands below its family peak; Fable's high/xhigh trade away landmines its own cheaper runs caught. The single counterexample is Codex-5.6 max (sweep peak, byte-exact, zero hygiene defects) — and even there, 2.8× the compute of high bought +0.20 composite.

08Recommendations

  1. Highest-stakes onboarding: Sonnet 5 at high effort. Still the top composite of all 36 runs (8.80), the only run combining a near-perfect KB with four clean, version-correct global promotions. Budget ~40 active CLI minutes; tidy the redundant skill cross-links.
  2. Best value: Sonnet 5 at medium (8.38 in ~26 min, the strongest quality-per-minute of any A-tier run), with Codex 5.6 at medium as the runner-up (8.20 in 35 min / 85K tokens — the cheapest tokens in the new wave). Neither promotes globally; add an explicit promotion pass.
  3. The effort dial: stop at each family's peak — never xhigh. No family peaks at the new xhigh tier, and only Codex 5.6 rewards max (8.55, first winning top-effort run — at 2.8× high's compute). Opus and Fable both peak at medium; Fable specifically loses at high/xhigh the very landmines its own low/medium caught, and its xhigh ships 4 sandbox-path leaks.
  4. When accuracy on API contracts matters most: Codex gpt-5.6-sol. The only family that catches the Db::Update inverted return at every effort, with zero fabricated APIs and mostly perfect link hygiene across ~250 files. Watch two spots: the high run's Test frameworks: jest line in AGENTS.md, and “MariaDB 10” harness branding in its taxonomy and 3 of its 6 promoted articles.
  5. When global routing matters: Opus 4.8 (promotes at every effort, xhigh included — audit for stray PHP7 APIs). The routing monopoly is broken though: Sonnet-4.6 xhigh, Fable high and Codex-5.6 high each promoted for the first time — single-run events, but proof the pipeline can route from any family.
  6. Do not use Haiku 4.5 for legacy onboarding. A full tier below (best 6.75): it reverses the Update contract, fabricates escaping, invents schema names, hallucinates a Jest agent at max — and at xhigh ships no testing doc at all while concretizing the fabricated $db->makeDBSafe() method.
▸ Methodology & caveats
  • Ground truth. All 36 runs onboarded byte-identical copies of the same Redaction System source (verified by content hash). Nine parallel auditors (six on July 13, three on July 14) judged each family's sweep against the real files and one shared, source-verified fact dossier (the Db::Update inverted return at database.php:456/459, the elocks edit-lock table, SECHASH=IP/UA not CSRF, no rank/CSRF API, encryptPW=md5(crypt), the eval() math-captcha) plus the earlier-scored runs as calibration anchors. Artifact count alone earned no credit.
  • Effort mechanism. Every run has its own CLI-provider row whose effort_level was verified in the database; claude-family efforts translate to the CLAUDE_CODE_EFFORT_LEVEL env var, Codex to -c model_reasoning_effort on the command line (visible in each run's stream log, including the gpt-5.6 values xhigh/max/ultra). The effect is corroborated by compute/output scaling. Models were verified from each run's CLI init log, not the task title: Opus 4.8 claude-opus-4-8, Sonnet 5 claude-sonnet-5, Sonnet 4.6 claude-sonnet-4-6, Haiku 4.5 claude-haiku-4-5, Fable 5 claude-fable-5, Codex gpt-5.5 and gpt-5.6-sol, GLM glm-5.2.
  • Detection (20%). Audited LLM output plus AGENTS.md/CLAUDE.md; deterministic confirmation-form values (mariadb 10.11, apache) were separated from model judgment, and copying them into LLM-authored KB was penalised.
  • KB (25%), Skills (25%), Agents (15%). Read every KB file (recursive), every SKILL.md, and only the accepted.source = "llm" discovered agents (the ~28 shared scan/template agents excluded, matching July-10). agentsDisc is the count of those llm-discovered agents from step 06_5.
  • Global KB (15%). Counted task-attributed promotions in haive_kb_global.global_kb_entries across every status; later archival by a newer run does not erase the originating decision. Bodies of promoted articles were read for portability + version-correctness. Zero promotions = the 6.0 neutral floor (no contamination, missed reuse), not a six-out-of-ten quality verdict.
  • Cost. Active CLI minutes = sum of cli_invocations.duration_ms across all rows incl. superseded retries; output tokens same rule. These exactly reproduce the July-10 published numbers for the six carried-over runs. Raw input/total tokens excluded (providers cache differently).
  • Wall time is contextual only and omitted from rankings: the three gpt-5.5 Codex runs sat ~40h at an unattended weekend form gate, and the Fable low/medium/high/max runs sat overnight (~8–15h wall) at the same kind of gate. Speed/value use active CLI minutes.
  • Calibration. Nine auditors scored family sweeps in two waves; each wave was reconciled against the previously published scores as anchors (e.g. one July-13 auditor flagged the calypso codename as an error until README:3 confirmed it is current — corrected; the July-14 Sonnet-5-xhigh score shape landing exactly on the Codex/GLM high anchor was treated as a coherence check, and the promotion-quality ladder — 1 clean entry 7.0, 3–4 clean 7.5–8.5, 5–6 mixed 8.0 — was applied uniformly across waves). Dimension scores are evidence-calibrated 0.5-point judgments, not measurements; composite decimals come only from the published weights.
